WeLearn

In 2019 I conducted a full redesign of Wirral Councils LMS: ULearn, introducing new functionality, improved UX and UI, and a repurposed catalogue of content spanning over 3000 items.

Benefits

  • Additional functionality was introduced including open badges, RSS feeds, notifications and a manager dashboard. Underused social functionality, surveys and feedback also began to be utilised.
  • WeLearn was redesigned with a human-centered approach to UX and UI, utilising a minimal, clean and vibrant design system as well as re-organised catalogue to reduce clicks.

Instructional Design Strategy

As the only officer responsible for learning, I developed a clear Instructional Design strategy and introduced a toolkit to support colleagues and SME's in delivering high impact learning experiences. The instructional toolkit contained a number of items, including:

  • Business Case Form
  • Learning theory (Gagne's Nine Events, Andragogy, Mayer's multimedia learning principles, etc.)
  • Templates for job, task, audience, cost/benefits analysis, etc.
  • Storyboards
  • Initial Design document templates
  • Database of tools and resources

Adaptive Hypermedia

Courses that adapt their structure and/or presentation to meet the needs of the learner.

Benefits

  • The curriculum is dynamic and can be re-sequenced based on exisiting data held on role, experience or preferences, etc. (using agent profile API). Sequencing can additionally be triggered on-the-fly as and when it receives data, i.e. knowledge check.

Remobilisation App

A native application which allowed staff to book desks, check-in to buildings, view up-to-date info on buildings and learn on the go.

Benefits

  • Learning can happen on location whilst the learner works.
  • Learning is delivered in bitesized pieces.
  • Learning is made more navigable via the search function.

Manager Essentials Game

A game which allows managers to practise essential skills in a simulated environment, guiding them through various scenarios that they may come across in the workplace.

Benefits

  • Learners are immersed in a story, interwoven with scenarios from the workplace and the odd unexpected emergency.
  • Learning is supported by various game mechanic; used to improve engagement and encourage re-playability
  • Work tasks are simulated and practise is encouraged in a safe environment.

5 minute lessons

A collection of courses split into lessons and delivered via email and SMS.

Benefits

  • Lessons are designed to last 5 minutes (approx.) making effective use of a learners time.
  • Delivery is automated and pushes out lessons at spaced intervals based on a defined pathway; allowing for improved retrieval.
  • Learning is scaffolded; building on prior experience and introducing opportunities to practise and explore related resources.
  • Courses and intervals can be chosen by the learner to fit in with their needs and schedule.

Virtual Agent

A conversational AI which can facilite personalised, automated interactions with users; assisting in system adoption and pushing relevant resources to users.

Benefits

  • The NLU is able to extract meaning from messages, determining intent as well as retaining important information for later use. The agent uses machine learning and generates training data on-the-fly, learning from the user over time.
  • The LOR can be accessed directly by the agent, allowing for indexing of learning content and delivery of resources to the learner at the point of need.

Evaluation System

A system for capturing feedback following learning events.

Benefits

  • The system ensures a zero-hassle approach to capturing learner feedback. With a quick scan of a QR code and the entry of a payroll no. they can easily enter their feedback.
  • Evaluations are spread out over time focusing on an implementation of the Kirkpatrick Model, capturing feedback pre and post course as well as behaviour/performance data in the months following.

Augmented Reality

Experiments in Mixed Realities.

Benefits

  • Learning is delivered in the Flow of work, at the point of need.
  • Augmented reality learning embeds digital learning content in the real world.

Process based learning

Learning that is designed to guide a learner through a paticular process, often with the generation of performance supports or documents to take away.

Benefits

  • Learning is scaffolded with learners supported step-by-step as they work through a process. Usually involves transferring classroom-based interactions or documents into a digital format.
  • Retrieval and reflection are supported with the use of performances supports, removing the need to complete the training again.

Wellbeing Microsite

A microsite for the delivery of wellbeing resources.

Benefits

  • Content discovery is supported by the organisation of learning into categories and search.

LXP

A microsite for the delivery of wellbeing resources.

Benefits

  • Flo strives to be personal, utilising an intelligent recommendation engine powered by machine learning, allowing for the personalisation and recommendation of content that fits the learners needs, skills, and interests. Flo will build up an intelligent profile which will adapt and change over time.
  • Flo pulls in content created and curated by the L&I team and subject matter experts from across the Council. Not only that, it also supports UGC, empowering learners to create and share their own content.
